Hidden ownership traps

Modern cars do not fail like old cars. They fail through systems.

The bundle is built around the ownership traps that hide behind a clean listing, a normal-looking dashboard, or a service advisor saying, "we recommend..."

01

OEM repair decisions

Check whether the design makes normal maintenance simple, expensive, shop-limited, or awkward enough that owners skip it.

02

Dealer-only paths

Security gateways, programming, calibrations, resets, VIN-locked modules, and brand tools can change the real cost of ownership.

03

Software lockouts

Apps, subscriptions, keys, accounts, battery registration, feature transfer, and service resets can follow the owner, not just the car.

04

Parts access risk

A cheap car is not cheap if common parts are backordered, dealer-only, unusually expensive, or tied to programming.

05

Diagnostics and calibration

One warning light can involve scan data, measured values, relearns, ADAS calibration, module setup, and proof that the first quote is the right quote.

06

Model-year landmines

The same nameplate can hide a risky redesign, weak drivetrain, expensive trim package, scarce electronics, or resale penalty.
